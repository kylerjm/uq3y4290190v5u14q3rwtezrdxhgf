What is Ausbildung in Germany?

Ausbildung is Germany’s dual vocational training system that combines real-world company work (3–4 days/week) with classroom education at a vocational school (1–2 days/week).

Trainees receive a monthly allowance and gain job-ready skills in high-demand industries.

Who Can Apply for Ausbildung in Germany?

  • Completed secondary or tertiary education
  • German language level B1–B2
  • Willingness to live and work in Germany
  • Eligible for training visa

Guide to Studying and Training in Germany

Step 1: Learn the German Language

The first and most important step is learning German. Since Ausbildung combines classroom learning with hands-on training, you'll need at least a B1 level of proficiency, with some programs requiring B2.

Not sure where to start? Learn German PH offers courses from A1 to B2, designed to help you pass certification exams like Goethe or Telc. Mastering German is your gateway to Ausbildung success.

Step 2: Check If You’re Eligible

High School Graduates – You can apply right after finishing high school.
College Undergraduates – Switch career paths.
Career Shifters – You can train in a different field.
No matter your background, there’s an Ausbildung program for you.

Step 3: Choose Your Ausbildung Course

Germany offers over 300 Ausbildung programs across various industries. Healthcare, IT & Technology, Hospitality & Culinary, Business & Administration. Think about your interests and career goals when choosing your Ausbildung course.

Step 4: Find an Ausbildung Employer

Search job portals like Make it in Germany, Bundesagentur für Arbeit, and Indeed.de.
Work with agencies that specialize in Ausbildung placements.
Network with people who have already gone through the process.
Once you find a company, you’ll receive an Ausbildungsvertrag (training contract), required for your visa application.

Step 5: Prepare Your Documents

German Language Certificate (B1/B2), High School Diploma (Apostilled & Translated), Updated CV (German format), Motivation Letter (Anschreiben), Passport & Photos, Visa Documents. Double-check specific program requirements.

Step 6: Apply for Your Visa

Once you have your Ausbildung contract and documents ready, apply for a German National Visa at the German Embassy. Apply early, as processing times can vary.

Step 7: Move to Germany & Begin Your Ausbildung!

Once your visa is approved, pack your bags. Ausbildung programs last 2–3.5 years. After completion, you can further your studies or seek employment opportunities in Germany.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Ausbildung?

It is a paid vocational training system in Germany that combines work and study.

How long does Ausbildung take?

Typically 2 to 3.5 years depending on the program and industry.

Do I get paid during Ausbildung?

Yes, trainees receive a monthly salary ranging from €900–€1,300.

Can I stay in Germany after completion?

Yes, many trainees receive job offers and can apply for residence permits.

What visa do I need?

You need a German National Visa for vocational training (Ausbildung visa).

Where do I learn German?

You can study at language schools, online courses, or private tutors.

Which sectors pay the best?

IT, Engineering, and Healthcare generally offer the highest monthly allowances.

Can I apply from my home country?

Yes, applications are accepted internationally before you arrive in Germany.

How do I prepare my CV?

Use the German CV format and highlight education, experience, and skills.

What happens if I fail a module?

Most programs allow a retake; consult your vocational school or employer for details.

Can I switch Ausbildung programs?

Yes, but it requires approval from your current employer and vocational school.

Are there part-time options?

Most programs are full-time, but some allow flexible schedules depending on the employer.
